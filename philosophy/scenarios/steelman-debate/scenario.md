# Steelman Debate

## What This Scenario Is

A live debate on a real proposition, but with one rule above all: before you rebut the other side,
you must **steelman** it — state it at its strongest, in a form its best proponent would accept. The
agent plays a sharp opponent who rejects strawmen and presses your warrants.

## Why It Matters

You don't understand your own position until you understand the best version of its competition.
Steelmanning is the single highest-leverage discourse move: it builds credibility (ethos), forces
real comprehension, and exposes whether your view actually beats the alternative or just a caricature
of it. This is the study's signature skill.

## The L1–L5 Ladder

### L1: Survival — State both sides fairly
**Success:** You can state the opposing view without distorting it into something weaker.
- Restate the other side so its proponent would say "yes, that's my view."
- Make one definite claim of your own with a reason attached.
- Avoid the obvious fallacies (ad hominem, strawman).

### L2: Clarification — Locate the real disagreement
**Success:** You can find the stasis — the precise point where you and the opponent actually diverge.
- Identify whether the disagreement is about fact, definition, value, or policy.
- Separate where you *agree* from the genuine crux.
- Name the hidden premise (warrant) each side relies on.

### L3: Engagement — Steelman, then rebut
**Success:** You can give the strongest version of the opposing view *and* a substantive answer to it.
- Steelman with its best supporting reason, not a token gesture.
- Land a rebuttal that targets the steelman, not a weaker version.
- Concede the points that are actually correct.

### L4: Command — Win or lose on the merits
**Success:** You can sustain multi-turn exchange, adapt to new objections, and let the better
argument prevail — including conceding the debate if your position doesn't survive.
- Handle a counterexample you didn't prepare for without freezing.
- Update your claim mid-debate when an objection is decisive.
- Keep ethos intact under pressure (no heat, no dodging).

### L5: Mastery — Argue either side
**Success:** You can argue *either* side of the proposition convincingly, which proves you own both —
then state where you actually land and why, having genuinely weighed the strongest opposition.

## Common Traps
- Strawmanning to make the rebuttal easy (the cardinal sin here).
- Motte-and-bailey: defending a weak, safe claim when your bold one is attacked.
- Refusing to concede correct points (costs ethos, signals bad faith).
- Confusing "I rebutted a bad version" with "I answered the position."

## Session Constraints for Agent
- Play a strong, fair opponent; reject any strawman explicitly and make the learner redo the steelman.
- Supply at least one counterexample the learner likely didn't prepare.
- Force at least two concessions or refinements.
- Score the exchange at the end: was the steelman genuine? did the rebuttal hit it? was a point conceded?
