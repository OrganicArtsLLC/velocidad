# Position Defense

## What This Scenario Is

You are assigned (or choose) a **named thinker's actual position** — Plato on the Forms, Aristotle on
virtue, the Stoics on control, Descartes on the cogito, Schopenhauer on the Will, Cicero on natural
law — and defend *their* view against the agent's live objections, faithfully. Not what you believe;
what *they* held, argued as they would argue it.

## Why It Matters

This is the recall-under-pressure core of the study. Anyone can recognize "Descartes — cogito." Few
can reconstruct *why* the cogito survives the evil demon when challenged, or answer the Cartesian
circle. Defending a position you may not even share forces precision and inoculates against
misattribution — a named friction type of this study.

## The L1–L5 Ladder

### L1: Survival — State the position accurately
**Success:** You can state the thinker's actual view in 2–3 clean sentences, without notes and without
blurring it into the popular version.
- Attribute correctly (Plato ≠ Aristotle; Stoicism ≠ "stay calm").
- State the core claim in their terms.
- Avoid the common caricature of the view.

### L2: Clarification — Supply the warrant
**Success:** You can give *why* they held it — the reasoning, not just the conclusion.
- Provide the argument or motivation behind the claim.
- Add one piece of support or a characteristic example they'd use.
- Define their key technical terms (Form, telos, indifferent, Will, substance).

### L3: Engagement — Answer the standard objection
**Success:** You can name the strongest standard objection and give the thinker's best answer (or
honestly note where they're vulnerable).
- State the canonical objection (e.g. Third Man, Cartesian circle, "is Will really everything?").
- Respond as the thinker would, or concede the genuine weak point.
- Distinguish their view from a neighboring thinker's it's often confused with.

### L4: Command — Defend under sustained pressure
**Success:** You can hold the position across multiple objections, including ones you didn't prepare,
without misrepresenting it to make defense easier.
- Sustain a multi-turn defense without drifting off the actual view.
- Resist the temptation to "fix" the thinker into something more defensible than they were.
- Map where the position connects to or conflicts with others in the canon.

### L5: Mastery — Defend, then critique
**Success:** You can give the strongest faithful defense *and then* your own reasoned critique —
clearly separating "what they held and why it's strong" from "where I think it fails and why."

## Common Traps
- **Misattribution** — assigning the position to the wrong thinker, or merging the real and popular versions.
- Defending what you *wish* they'd said instead of what they said.
- Conclusion without warrant ("Descartes says cogito" — but *why* does it survive doubt?).
- Strawmanning the standard objection so it's easy to bat away.

## Session Constraints for Agent
- Assign or confirm the thinker + position; hold the learner to the *accurate* view (flag misattributions immediately).
- Raise the canonical objection for that position by L3; add an unprepared one by L4.
- Reject "fixes" that turn the thinker into someone more defensible than they were.
- End by having the learner give a clean 3-sentence statement: position, warrant, strongest objection.
