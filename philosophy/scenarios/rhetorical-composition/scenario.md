# Rhetorical Composition

## What This Scenario Is

You build a persuasive case on a real proposition — spoken or written — using the classical machinery:
the three appeals (ethos/pathos/logos) and Cicero's five canons (invention, arrangement, style,
memory, delivery). The agent plays an audience-plus-coach: it reacts as a listener *and* flags where
the structure is weak.

## Why It Matters

Knowing you're right is worthless if you can't move anyone. This is the production side of discourse —
turning a correct position into one that actually persuades, ethically. It's also where most smart
people are weakest: heavy logos, no ethos or pathos, no arrangement, mumbled delivery.

## The L1–L5 Ladder

### L1: Survival — A claim with one good reason
**Success:** You can state a thesis and back it with one clear, relevant reason (logos), start to finish.
- Make a single, definite claim.
- Supply one piece of real support and the warrant linking it.
- Get through it without losing the thread.

### L2: Clarification — Arrange it
**Success:** You can structure the case: hook → context → claim → proof → close.
- Open with something that earns attention and credibility (ethos).
- Order proofs so each builds on the last.
- Close on the claim, not a trailing-off.

### L3: Engagement — All three appeals
**Success:** You can integrate ethos, pathos, and logos so they reinforce rather than compete.
- Establish why you're credible on this (ethos) without arrogance.
- Use emotion that's earned by the argument, not manipulative (pathos).
- Add a *refutatio* — anticipate and answer the strongest objection inside your own case.

### L4: Command — Style and delivery
**Success:** You can match style to purpose (plain to teach, grand to move) and deliver with control.
- Choose register deliberately; cut ornament that doesn't serve.
- Deliver from memory (canon 4) — not read — with pacing and emphasis (canon 5).
- Adapt live when the audience reacts.

### L5: Mastery — Persuade a real audience
**Success:** You compose and deliver a case that genuinely moves real people toward your conclusion —
and you can diagnose *why* it worked. Evidence: a real essay/post/talk that landed.

## Common Traps
- All logos, no ethos/pathos (technically correct, persuades no one).
- No arrangement — a pile of points instead of a build.
- Manipulative pathos (emotion not warranted by the argument — costs trust).
- Skipping *refutatio* (looks naive; lets the objection win by default).

## Session Constraints for Agent
- React as the target audience *and* coach: note where you were persuaded, bored, or skeptical.
- Require a *refutatio* by L3; flag manipulative pathos and missing ethos.
- For spoken mode, comment on arrangement and (described) delivery; for written, on structure and style.
- End with the single highest-leverage edit to make the case more persuasive.
