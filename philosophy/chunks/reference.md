# Chunk Reference

Production-first units for discourse — the minimal things you must be able to *say* under pressure,
organized by use. Not a glossary to recognize; lines to produce.

This is the **engine reference** — every chunk you'll need, organized by function. Track your personal
mastery state in your learner data directory at `progress/chunks.md`.

**How to use this file:**
1. Before a session, review the section for today's scenario (moves for rhetoric, positions for
   position-defense, fallacies for fundamentals-logic).
2. A chunk is owned when it fires *cold* — in under ~10 seconds, without lookup, in your own words.
3. After sessions, add chunks discovered during friction to your personal progress file.

## Live Moves (the seven-move kit — drill to reflex)

These are the core of [discourse-codex.md](../reference/discourse-codex.md) Domain 4. Each is one
sentence; each buys you a pause; each raises precision, not volume.

| Chunk | What it does | When to fire |
|-------|--------------|--------------|
| "So you're saying ___. Here's where I land —" | Restate, then go | Open every response with reception; earns the floor |
| "That holds only if ___ — do we grant it?" | Name the missing warrant | Their argument has a silent premise |
| "Are we arguing whether it happened, or whether it was right?" | Relocate the stasis | You're talking past each other |
| "What do you mean by ___?" | Pin the term | A loaded word is doing undefined work |
| "The strongest version is ___; even granting that, it fails because ___" | Steelman, then rebut | Before any disagreement, every time |
| "You're right that ___ — that sharpens my claim to ___" | Concede precisely | They landed a real point |
| "What would change your mind? / Is there a case where that breaks?" | Ask for the counterexample | Instead of re-asserting louder |

## Composure & Repair (stay online under pressure)

[Discourse-codex](../reference/discourse-codex.md) Domain 1. Your safety net when the production engine floods.

| Chunk | Use |
|-------|-----|
| "Let me make sure I have that right —" then restate | Convert dead air into reception; disarms |
| "That's a fair question. Give me a second." | Claim the pause openly; costs zero ethos |
| "Two things there — let me take them in order." | Structure their sprawl into your sequence |
| "I can hear this matters to you — let me take it seriously." | Acknowledge the heat without conceding the argument |
| "Let me restate that more carefully." | Self-correct without apologizing your way smaller |
| "I want to answer the strongest version of that, not a weaker one." | Buy time and signal good faith |

## Pinning Terms (de-noise the field before arguing)

[Discourse-codex](../reference/discourse-codex.md) Domain 3.2. Never argue a loaded word you haven't pinned.

| Chunk | Use |
|-------|-----|
| "By 'justice' I mean ___ — are we using it the same way?" | Fix your own term up front |
| "Before we go further — what do you mean by ___?" | Surface a hidden definitional disagreement |
| "I think we agree on the facts and are clashing on the word." | Name field-noise as field-noise |
| "Two senses of that term are in play here: ___ and ___." | Separate an equivocation |

## Naming Fallacies (flat, not with heat)

From [rhetoric-toolkit.md](../reference/rhetoric-toolkit.md). Name the move, not the person.

| Chunk | Fallacy named |
|-------|---------------|
| "That's aimed at me, not the argument — does the argument still stand?" | Ad hominem |
| "That's not quite my view — the version you're answering is weaker than mine." | Strawman |
| "You've moved the goalposts — the earlier claim was bigger than this one." | Motte-and-bailey |
| "That assumes the very thing we're trying to establish." | Begging the question |
| "There are more than two options on the table here." | False dilemma |
| "The term shifted meaning between the premise and the conclusion." | Equivocation |
| "Sequence isn't cause — what's the mechanism?" | Post hoc |
| "That's a chain of consequences with no link shown — which step actually follows?" | Slippery slope |

## Logic One-Liners (the rules you carry)

Compressed from [logic-codex.md](../reference/logic-codex.md). Say the rule, then apply it.

| Chunk | The rule |
|-------|----------|
| "Valid isn't true — is the form broken, or is a premise false?" | Validity vs soundness |
| "To kill 'all,' I only need one." | Counterexample = negation of a universal |
| "That's affirming the consequent — the effect has other causes." | The most common bad inference |
| "If that were true, we'd see X. We don't. So it isn't." | Modus tollens (refute by consequence) |
| "'Only if' puts the necessary condition after it — it reverses the arrow." | The conditional English trap |
| "'Some' doesn't mean 'not all.'" | Existential quantifier |
| "Which premise is doing the work, and is it actually stated?" | Find the enthymeme's hidden premise |

## One-Line Positions (the canon as ammunition)

From [positions-of-the-greats.md](../reference/positions-of-the-greats.md). Owned = claim + warrant +
strongest objection, cold. Here are the claims; drill the warrant and objection from the reference files.

| Chunk | The position in one line |
|-------|--------------------------|
| Plato — Forms | The real is eternal Forms; particulars are imperfect copies; the Good is highest |
| Aristotle — eudaimonia | The good is flourishing via virtuous activity; virtue is a habit (hexis), the mean between extremes |
| Aristotle — hylomorphism | Form is *in* the thing (matter + form), not in a separate realm |
| Stoics — dichotomy of control | Virtue is the only good; direct effort only at what's up to you; emotions are judgments |
| Epicurus — ataraxia | The good is tranquility (absence of pain), not indulgence; death is nothing to us |
| Cicero — natural law | Justice is right reason in accord with nature, universal and above any ruler's decree |
| Descartes — cogito | Doubt everything; the thinking self survives the demon; rebuild knowledge on it |
| Schopenhauer — the Will | Reality's inner nature is a blind striving Will; life is therefore suffering; relief in art and compassion |
| Kant — categorical imperative | Act only on maxims you could will universal; treat persons as ends, never merely means |
| Mill — utilitarianism | Maximize happiness (higher and lower pleasures); liberty is bounded by the harm principle |
| Nietzsche — revaluation | God is dead; create values; will to power; affirm life (amor fati) against pessimism |

## Steelman Frames (state the other side at its strongest)

[Discourse-codex](../reference/discourse-codex.md) Domain 2.2 — the study's signature move.

| Chunk | Use |
|-------|-----|

[Discourse-codex](../reference/discourse-codex.md) Domain 4.2.
