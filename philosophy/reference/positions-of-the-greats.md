# Positions of the Greats — One-Line Index

The SRS seedbed: each thinker's **actual** position in one line, plus the strongest standing
objection. "Front" of a card = the thinker (or the claim); "Back" = the position + warrant. Drill
both directions: *given the thinker, state the position* and *given the position, name the thinker*.

The **core set** (a sensible place to start) is marked ★. The rest are the surrounding canon —
the "all the greats" you grow into.

*Original educational summary (CC BY-SA 4.0). Dates and attributions reflect standard scholarship;
where dating is approximate it is marked "c."*

| Thinker | Era | Position in one line | Strongest objection |
|---------|-----|----------------------|---------------------|
| Heraclitus | c. 500 BCE | Reality is flux ("you can't step in the same river twice"); unity in opposites, governed by Logos | If all flows, how is knowledge or identity possible? (Parmenides' challenge) |
| Parmenides | c. 500 BCE | Change is illusion; what *is*, is — being is one, eternal, unchanging | Flatly denies the world we observe |
| Socrates ★ | 470–399 BCE | Virtue is knowledge; "the unexamined life is not worth living"; I know that I know nothing | Did he have positive doctrines or only the elenchus? |
| Plato ★ | c. 428–348 BCE | Reality is eternal **Forms**; the Good is highest; justice = reason ruling the soul/city; philosopher-kings | Third Man regress; political elitism |
| Aristotle ★ | 384–322 BCE | Forms are *in* things; the good is **eudaimonia** via virtuous habit + the golden mean; man is a political animal | Teleology dispensable post-Darwin; natural slavery |
| Epicurus | 341–270 BCE | The good is pleasure rightly understood (ataraxia — tranquility, absence of pain), not indulgence; death is nothing to us | Is a life aimed at tranquility too thin? |
| Zeno / Epictetus ★ | c. 300 BCE / c. 55–135 CE | **Stoicism**: virtue is the only good; master the dichotomy of control; live according to nature/Logos | Are externals really "indifferent"? Looks too austere |
| Cicero ★ | 106–43 BCE | Justice is **natural law** (right reason in accord with nature); the republic belongs to the people; the five canons of rhetoric | Assumes a normative, rational cosmos |
| Seneca ★ | c. 4 BCE–65 CE | Life is long enough if not wasted; hold externals loosely; rehearse adversity (Stoicism in practice) | His wealth vs. his creed |
| Marcus Aurelius ★ | 121–180 CE | Even an emperor governs only his own judgment; "the obstacle is the way"; duty to the common good | Private journal, not a system |
| Augustine | 354–430 CE | Faith seeking understanding; the will is divided; time is in the mind; original sin | Depends on Christian premises |
| Aquinas | 1225–1274 | Reason and faith harmonize; natural law as participation in eternal law; Five Ways for God | The Five Ways are widely contested |
| Descartes ★ | 1596–1650 | Doubt all → **cogito ergo sum**; rebuild knowledge on the thinking self by reason; mind/body dualism | Cartesian circle; mind–body interaction |
| Spinoza | 1632–1677 | One substance — God *or* Nature (*Deus sive Natura*); freedom is understanding necessity | Determinism leaves little room for agency |
| Hume | 1711–1776 | Empiricism + skepticism: no rational basis for causation/induction; "is" can't yield "ought"; the self is a bundle | Is global skepticism livable? |
| Kant | 1724–1804 | We structure experience (space/time/categories); morality = the **categorical imperative** (act on universalizable maxims; treat persons as ends) | The noumenal/thing-in-itself is unknowable yet posited |
| Schopenhauer ★ | 1788–1860 | Reality's inner nature is a blind **Will**; life is therefore suffering; relief in art and **compassion** | "My inner will" → "all is Will" is a leap |
| Hegel | 1770–1831 | History is Spirit (Geist) unfolding rationally via **dialectic** toward freedom | Is the system's necessity real or imposed? |
| Mill | 1806–1873 | **Utilitarianism**: maximize happiness (higher vs. lower pleasures); the harm principle bounds liberty | Can justice/rights be reduced to aggregate utility? |
| Nietzsche | 1844–1900 | God is dead; revalue values; **will to power**; affirm life (amor fati) against Schopenhauer's pessimism | Where does the new value-standard come from? |

> **Politics cross-cut** (for the `steelman-debate` / `position-defense` scenarios): the classic
> regime typology runs Plato → Aristotle → Polybius/Cicero. Correct forms (one/few/many ruling for the
> common good: monarchy / aristocracy / polity) vs. their corruptions (tyranny / oligarchy /
> mob-democracy). The **mixed constitution** (balance the three) is an ancestor of separation of powers.
> Modern social-contract answers (Hobbes: security via the sovereign; Locke: consent + natural rights;
> Rousseau: the general will) are the next layer to add.
